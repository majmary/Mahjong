FAQ

Each player starts with 500 points. When someone wins a hand, every other player pays them the value of the winning hand — but the amount you owe depends on how the winner went out.

Hand values are set by the NMJL card. Each hand shows either X (open — exposures allowed) or C (closed — no exposures), followed by its point value. For example, X 25 means an open hand worth 25 points, and C 30 means a closed hand worth 30.

Discard win: The player who threw the winning tile pays double the hand's point value. Everyone else pays the base value.

Self-draw (wall): The winner drew their own winning tile from the wall. All three opponents each pay double the hand's point value.

Joker-free bonus: If the winner completed their hand without using any jokers (and it isn't a Singles & Pairs hand), all payments are doubled again.

The session ends when any player's score reaches zero — that player loses. The other three players are the non-losers. There's no single winner in Mahjong, just one person left holding an empty wallet.

The Charleston is a tile-passing ritual that happens after the deal, before play begins. It gives everyone a chance to improve their hand.

In the first Charleston, you pass three tiles to the right, then three across, then three to the left. The final pass (to the left) is "blind" — you can pass back tiles you just received without looking at them.

After the first Charleston, players may choose to stop or continue with a second Charleston. If everyone agrees to continue, the order reverses: left, across, right. The final pass (to the right) is again blind.

Once all passing is complete, there is an optional courtesy pass — a one-time opportunity to trade 1–3 tiles with the player directly across from you, but only if both players want to.

Jokers are wild tiles that can substitute for any tile in a group of three or more identical tiles — pungs (3 of a kind), kongs (4 of a kind), and larger same-tile groups. That's the full extent of their power.

Jokers cannot be used in runs or sequences (like N-E-W-S or 2-4-6-8), as part of a pair, or as a single tile. This means jokers are completely useless in the Singles & Pairs section — which is a big part of why those hands carry the highest point values on the card.

Jokers also cannot be called from the discard pile. If someone discards a joker, it's gone — no one can claim it.

During play, if you hold a real tile that matches one a joker is standing in for in another player's exposed set, you can swap your tile for their joker on your turn — this is called a joker exchange.

Winning without any jokers in your hand earns the joker-free bonus, which doubles all payments you receive.

If all the tiles in the wall are drawn and no one has declared Mahjong, the round ends in a draw (also called a "wall game"). No points change hands. The same dealer deals again.
